## Design Context

### Users
Tourists visiting Jingchu (Hubei region) who scan QR codes at cultural sites, as well as online visitors interested in Chinese character art and Jingchu cultural heritage. They are looking for a visually engaging, educational, and modern interpretation of historical text IPs.

### Brand Personality
Elegant, historical yet modern, dramatic, and refined. Think "digital museum" meets "contemporary art gallery."
Keywords: Cultural, Immersive, Elegant, Bold.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual Tone**: High-contrast, editorial/magazine style. We will use a "Digital Gallery" aesthetic with generous negative space to let the character designs breathe.
- **Theme**: Can default to a sophisticated light theme (off-white/paper background like `#f9f8f6`) with deep ink black (`#1a1a1a`) and accents of Chu Red (朱砂红 `#c9372c`) and Bronze (`#4a5d4e`).
- **Typography**: A striking Serif font for Chinese characters and headings (e.g., Noto Serif SC or ZCOOL XiaoWei) paired with a clean, geometric sans-serif for body text and UI controls. No generic default fonts.
- **Motion**: Deliberate, staggered reveals. Exponential easing. Elements should fade and slide up gracefully as if being curated onto the screen.

### Design Principles
1. **Content is Art**: The characters are the heroes. UI should recede and frame the content like a gallery wall.
2. **Tactile & Crafted**: Avoid flat, lifeless layouts. Use subtle textures, borders, and typography scales that feel editorial and crafted.
3. **Fluid Rhythm**: Use modular typography and fluid spacing. Break the grid for featured IPs to create an engaging visual rhythm.
4. **No AI Slop**: Absolutely no decorative side-stripes, no gradient text, no meaningless glassmorphism. Use solid colors and intentional typography for hierarchy.
